Sauerkraut

This recipe is adapted from  Natasha's Kitchen

This tastes nice on its own but I prefer it served as Sauerkraut Apple Salad (see below).

Ingredients

  • 1 medium/large cabbage (the lighter in colour the better)
  • 2 carrots, grated
  • 1 TBSP fine sea salt
  • 1 1/2 tsp sugar

Method

  1. Remove outer leaves of cabbage, quarter and finely shred (using a mandolin is easiest). Discard the core.
  2. Place the cabbage, grated carrot, sugar and salt into a large bowl. Scrunch and knead the ingredients together for around 5 minutes until lots of water is released.
  3. Fill a glass jar with the cabbage mixture, pushing it in tightly so the cabbage is all covered by the liquid. Ensure the jar is only around 2/3 full to give the mixture room to expand.
  4. Make a press over the cabbage using a small plate or lid and weight it down with a jar filled with water or a clean rock.
  5. Put a lid on the jar but do not tighten (I remove the rubber seal from a kilner jar).
  6. Let stand at room temperature for 4 days until sour, it will stop rising / fermenting when its done.
  7. Each day while it is fermenting poke a few holes into the cabbage with the back of a wooden spoon to allow the air bubbles to release. Press the cabbage back down tightly.
  8. After 4 days, refrigerate until ready to serve.

Sauerkraut Apple Salad

Mix together:

  • Half a red onion
  • half an apple
  • 1/2 tsp sugar
  • small handful of grapes, sliced
  • 1 - 2 TBSP olive oil
  • a few handfuls of sauerkraut, drained
